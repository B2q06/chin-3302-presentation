Part 01 · Tang Dynasty · 618–907 CE

Tang Dynasty: 飞钱 "Flying Money"

  • The Problem: Transporting physical metal across long distances was dangerous (bandits).
  • The Innovation: The 飞钱 (Fēiqián) system — an early Bill of Exchange.
  • The Mechanism: Deposit cash in the capital; receive a voucher to redeem at home.
  • The Leap: Value begins to travel faster than physical objects.

元代纸币 · Yuan Dynasty Paper Money

The precedent: Value can move as information, not just as physical material. This is the DNA of every stock exchange.

Part 01 · Song Dynasty · ~1024 CE

The 交子 (Jiāozǐ) — The World's First "Stock"

  • The Context: A metal shortage in Sichuan leads to private "Promissory Notes."
  • The Jiaozi: State-authorized paper receipts backed by iron/copper reserves.
  • Market Logic: People begin trading the receipts instead of the metal.
  • The Modern Link: The Jiaozi is a "Derivative" — trading a promise of ownership.

The world's first paper money — 600 years before Europe. When people trade the promise instead of the thing, you have a stock market in embryo.

The Big Picture

Why Does China's Market Feel Like a Casino?

STRUCTURE

Retail drives 80–90% of volume. No short sellers to correct overvaluation. Daily limits trap investors in bad positions.

CULTURE

炒股 (chǎo gǔ) = stir-fry. 韭菜 (jiǔcài) = leeks. Gambling is culturally normalized. The market is seen as fate and luck — not fundamentals.

INFORMATION

No independent Wall Street. Retail follows forum Big Vs, "stock gods," and government signals instead of earnings.

THE STATE

The government is both the regulator AND the biggest whale. The market is a policy tool — not a free capital market.
